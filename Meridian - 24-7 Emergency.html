What counts as an emergency

If any of these is happening, come in now.

Not sure how serious it is? Call and describe it — our team will tell you honestly whether it's tonight or tomorrow. When in doubt, we'd rather see you.

Knocked-out tooth

A fully knocked-out adult tooth can often be saved — but the window is short. Keep it in milk or saliva and head straight here.

Act within 60 minutes

Facial swelling

Swelling in the face, jaw or under the eye can signal a spreading infection. This is urgent — don't wait for morning.

Treat tonight

Severe, throbbing pain

Pain that keeps you awake, won't settle with painkillers, or pulses with your heartbeat usually means the nerve is involved.

Same-hour relief

Broken or chipped tooth

A sharp edge cutting your tongue, or a large fracture exposing the inside of the tooth, needs smoothing or sealing quickly.

Come in today

Lost crown or filling

An exposed tooth can be sensitive and fragile. We can re-cement or temporarily protect it so it doesn't break further.

Quick re-fix

Bleeding that won't stop

Ongoing bleeding after an extraction or injury, or bleeding gums with pain, should be checked rather than left overnight.

Before you come in

Quick answers

Do I really need an appointment?
No. You can walk in at any hour. A quick call ahead just lets us prepare the right room and have a dentist ready the moment you arrive.
Are you actually open through the night?
Yes — a dentist and support team are physically on site 24 hours a day, every day of the year, including festivals and public holidays.
How much will it cost?
The emergency visit fee of ₹600 covers your exam and X-ray. Any treatment is quoted clearly afterwards, and nothing begins until you've approved the price.
My tooth got knocked out — what do I do right now?
Pick it up by the crown (not the root), gently rinse it without scrubbing, and keep it in milk or your own saliva. Come in within the hour for the best chance of saving it.
Can you treat children?
Yes. We see children of all ages for knocks, falls, swelling and toothache, with a calm, gentle approach designed to keep them at ease.
Do you accept insurance?
We offer cashless treatment with major insurers and no-cost EMI on treatments above ₹8,000. Bring your policy details and we'll handle the paperwork.
